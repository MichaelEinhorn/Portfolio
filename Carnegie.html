Carnegie Mellon: National High School Game Academy

Attended a 6-week Computer Science course at Carnegie Mellon for game design. Collaborative teams of 5 people were assigned to remake a classic game with a twist, and then make an original idea in VR. Learned how to maintain focus and motivation within the team as the stress of the deadline approached and how to manage code with multiple people. Learned to use Audacity for sound design, photoshop, and Autodesk Maya, while programming the game with the Unity engine in C#.

We made an original game in VR in Unity 3D about swatting bees using various items around the room. One of the features I developed was the movement of the bees buzzing by switching between different circles while aproaching the player. This added more life to the game then just moving in a straight line.

We made a remake of Zelda II in Unity 3D and rethemed it to be an Astronaut fighting aliens who made a Dyson Sphere around the sun. One of the features I developed was collision detection with the shield to prevent damage to the player.
