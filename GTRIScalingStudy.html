Georgia Tech Research Institute CIPHER

In the fall of my Junior Year, I was a full-time intern at Georgia Tech Research Institute where I researched scaling laws for convolutional networks trained on a small image dataset. I experimented with the effect of data augmentation on the scaling law, which is the relation between the quantity of data and the performance of the model. An accurate measure of scaling laws allows for predicting what quantity of data is needed to achieve a certain performance level. I trained multiple models and used regression to statistically test for a change in the exponent of the scaling law. I taught myself a new coding language, R, to run this analysis because I found that Python’s statistical tools were insufficient. I found that Mixup, the data augmentation technique tested, increased the exponent of the scaling law meaning that less data was required to reach the same performance. I identified a problem, a tool that was able to solve it, independently learn how to use it, and wrote a report to share the results which can be found below.
